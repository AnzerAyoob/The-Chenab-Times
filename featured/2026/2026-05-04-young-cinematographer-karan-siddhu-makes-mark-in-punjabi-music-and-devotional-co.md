        # Young Cinematographer Karan Siddhu Makes Mark in Punjabi Music and Devotional Content

        **By News Desk CT | 2026-05-04**

        Jalandhar: Karandeep Singh, professionally known as Karan Siddhu, is steadily gaining recognition as a Director of Photography in the Punjabi entertainment industry. Born on May 18, 2004, in Jalandhar, Punjab, the young talent has worked on several music videos and devotional projects in a short span.



According to details received by The Chenab Times, Karan Siddhu has contributed to projects that blend technical skill with creative storytelling. Information was available with The Chenab Times about his growing portfolio in contemporary Punjabi content.



Karan Siddhu, who hails from Punjab, began showing interest in visual storytelling from an early age. His transition from personal passion to professional cinematography reflects a focused approach towards the craft. At present, he operates as a Director of Photography, also referred to as DOP, on various productions.



One of his recent works includes the song “Duaavan” by Sukh Chahal, released under Folk Studios. The project features Karan Siddhu’s cinematography, which adds visual depth to the track.







Earlier, he served as DOP on “Jeona Mour 2,” featuring veteran singer Surinder Shinda. The 2022 release combined traditional Punjabi storytelling elements with updated cinematic presentation. Karan Siddhu’s involvement in this project highlighted his ability to work across different styles within the Punjabi music space.



He also contributed to “Deewani Murli Wale Di” by Aar Bawa. The devotional track, a Krishna bhajan, required a sensitive approach to visuals. Karan Siddhu’s work on the project demonstrated his range beyond high-energy music videos into spiritual and reflective content.



Another project in his credits is “Rarhe Bhambiri Bole” by Rushil, featuring Babli Singh. Released in 2022, the track showcased vibrant visuals and dynamic camera movements typical of modern Punjabi songs. Karan Siddhu’s handling of compositions brought energy and clarity to the production.



Despite his young age, Karan Siddhu has shown versatility. His work spans soulful tracks, devotional pieces, and energetic music videos. He pays close attention to lighting, framing, and overall visual storytelling to support the core narrative of each song.







Karan Siddhu’s approach emphasizes both technical precision and artistic contribution. He adapts camera techniques according to the mood and theme of the content. This flexibility has allowed him to deliver consistent results across projects with different requirements.



The Punjabi music industry continues to evolve with new talents entering various roles, including cinematography. Karan Siddhu represents part of this younger generation that brings fresh perspectives while respecting established traditions. His projects connect with audiences through strong visual language that complements the audio elements.



His frames are emotionally resonant. The cinematographer focuses on creating visuals that enhance the viewer’s connection with the song’s message. Whether capturing intimate moments in devotional content or lively sequences in mainstream tracks, his style aims for authenticity and impact.



Karan Siddhu continues to build experience through hands-on projects. He experiments with new techniques and technologies available in the field. This commitment to learning supports his development as a professional in a competitive industry.







Born and raised in Jalandhar, Karan Siddhu maintains links to Punjab’s cultural roots. This background appears to influence his understanding of Punjabi narratives and audience expectations. His work often reflects an appreciation for both modern trends and traditional elements present in regional storytelling.



The field of cinematography in regional Indian cinema demands a balance of creativity and technical knowledge. Karan Siddhu has taken steps in this direction through consistent project deliveries. His portfolio includes collaborations with established names such as Surinder Shinda and emerging artists like Sukh Chahal.



As digital platforms expand the reach of Punjabi content, the role of visual professionals gains greater importance. Quality cinematography helps productions stand out in a crowded market. Karan Siddhu’s contributions align with this growing demand for polished and meaningful visuals.



Further details about his upcoming projects remain limited at this stage. However, his current body of work suggests a trajectory focused on steady growth. The young DOP continues to take on assignments that test and expand his skills.



Punjabi cinema and music have a rich history of visual innovation. New entrants like Karan Siddhu add to this legacy by introducing contemporary techniques while working within cultural contexts.
